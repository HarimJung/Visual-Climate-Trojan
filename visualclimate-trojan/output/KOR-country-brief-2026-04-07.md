## Emissions Profile

South Korea's total GHG emissions (excl. LULUCF) stood at 624.2 MtCO₂eq in 2023, down from 661.1 MtCO₂eq in 2013 (−5.6% over the decade) (OWID, GCP/EDGAR, 2023). CO₂ from fossil fuel combustion and industrial processes accounted for 589.2 MtCO₂, representing 94.4% of total GHG emissions (OWID, 2023). The fuel mix is dominated by coal (263.9 MtCO₂), oil (170.4 MtCO₂), and natural gas (122.1 MtCO₂), with cement contributing 22.7 MtCO₂ (OWID, 2023). Methane emissions totaled 36.5 MtCO₂eq (OWID, 2023). The 2018 reference year for Korea's NDC saw emissions of 700.4 MtCO₂eq, meaning the 2023 level represents an 10.9% reduction from the NDC baseline — but a further 29.5% reduction is needed to meet the 2030 target of 436.6 MtCO₂eq. Climate TRACE estimated Korea's total GHG at 709.2 MtCO₂eq for 2023 (CT.GHG.TOTAL), which is 13.6% higher than OWID's estimate — ⚠️ exceeding the 10% cross-check threshold. This divergence likely reflects methodological differences: Climate TRACE uses satellite-based bottom-up estimation, while OWID/GCP relies on top-down national reporting.

## Energy Transition

Korea's electricity sector remains heavily fossil-fuel dependent, with 61.8% of generation from fossil sources (Ember, 2023). Renewable energy contributed only 9.0% of electricity generation (Ember, 2023), far below the peer median of 34.9%. Over the past five years, the renewable share has increased by approximately 4.9 percentage points (Derived, EMBER, 2023), indicating gradual but insufficient progress. Korea's per capita energy consumption is 68,753 kWh equivalent (OWID, 2023), reflecting an energy-intensive industrial structure. The country has no coal phase-out commitment date, and continues to operate significant coal-fired power capacity. Reducing dependence on coal (which alone accounts for 263.9 MtCO₂) is the single largest decarbonization lever for Korea's power sector.

South Korea's 2030 NDC pledges a 40% absolute reduction from 2018 levels, targeting 436.6 MtCO₂eq (UNFCCC NDC Registry / Climate Watch). As of April 2026, Korea has not submitted an NDC 3.0 with a 2035 target. The country enacted its Carbon Neutrality and Green Growth Framework Act in 2021, legally enshrining a 2050 net-zero target. However, the Climate Action Tracker rates Korea's overall climate action as "Highly Insufficient," indicating that current policies and targets are not aligned with limiting warming to 1.5°C or even well below 2°C.
